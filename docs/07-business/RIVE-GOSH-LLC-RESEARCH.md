## Part 1: Delaware vs Wyoming

### Cost Comparison (Annual)

| Cost | Delaware | Wyoming | Savings |
|------|----------|---------|---------|
| Formation | $110 | $100 | $10 |
| Annual Tax/Report | $300 | $50 | $250 |
| Business License | $75 | $0 | $75 |
| **Total Year 1** | **$485** | **$150** | **$335** |
| **Total Annual** | **$375** | **$50** | **$325/yr** |

### Feature Comparison

| Feature | Delaware | Wyoming |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Privacy | Members disclosed in some filings | **Fully anonymous** |
| Asset Protection | Good | **Stronger (charging order only)** |
| Court System | Chancery Court (specialized) | Standard courts |
| Investor Appeal | **High (Fortune 500 reputation)** | Lower |
| No State Income Tax | ✅ (if no DE operations) | ✅ |
| Best For | VC-backed startups, IPO track | **Small businesses, privacy, cost** |

### My Recommendation: **Wyoming**

**Why:**
1. **$325/year cheaper** - over 10 years = $3,250 saved
2. **Stronger privacy** - member names not disclosed
3. **You don't need Delaware's benefits:**
   - Not raising VC (50/50 partnership)
   - Not planning IPO
   - Not needing specialized business courts
4. **Same federal tax treatment** - LLC is LLC regardless of state

## Part 2: US LLC vs French SAS

### Why NOT French SAS?

| Issue | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| French bureaucracy | Complex formation, ongoing compliance |
| Social charges | High employer contributions (~45%) |
| French tax authority | Aggressive, complex reporting |
| Client perception | US LLC = standard for international tech |

### Why US LLC (for your situation)?

| Benefit | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| International credibility | US LLC is recognized worldwide |
| Simple formation | 1-2 days, minimal paperwork |
| No French social charges | You're contractors, not employees |
| Payment flexibility | US bank account, international payments |
| You already have one | Badaboost LLC experience |

### Tax Reality for France Residents

**Critical Warning:** French CFC (Controlled Foreign Corporation) rules may apply.

| Rule | Implication |
|------|-------------|
| **CFC Threshold** | If you control >50% of foreign entity with tax rate <50% of French rate |
| **What happens** | France may tax the LLC's profits directly, even if not distributed |
| **Mitigation** | Document everything, proper accounting, consider French tax advisor |

**US Filing Requirements:**
- Form 5472 required for foreign-owned US LLC
- Penalty for missing: **$25,000 per year**
- Need US tax preparer familiar with foreign-owned LLCs

**Sources:**
- [Taxes for Expats: Foreign Company Reporting](https://www.taxesforexpats.com/articles/foreign-business/foreign-company-tax-reporting.html)
- [Claimyr: US LLC as French Citizen](https://claimyr.com/government-services/irs/Setting-up-a-US-LLC-as-a-French-citizen-tax-implications/2025-04-11)

## Part 3: Stéphane Compensation Options

### Why NOT Equity?

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| Complexity | 3-way split complicates decisions |
| Exit issues | What if Stéphane wants out? |
| Variable involvement | He's on some projects, not all |
| Cleaner books | 50/50 is simple, additions create mess |

### Compensation Alternatives

#### Option A: Per-Project Profit Share ← **Recommended**

```
For each project Stéphane works on:
├── Project Revenue: €10,000
├── Expenses: €2,000
├── Net Profit: €8,000
│
├── Roderic: €4,000 (50%)
├── Daniel: €4,000 (50%)
│
└── Stéphane: Paid from Daniel's share
    (Daniel and Stéphane sort it out internally)
```

**Pros:**
- Cleanest for LLC
- Stéphane only gets paid on projects he works on
- Daniel handles his own "side" of the partnership

**Cons:**
- Daniel's share is effectively reduced

#### Option B: Revenue Share per Project

| Project Role | Stéphane Gets |
|--------------|---------------|
| Market access only (intros) | 5-10% of project revenue |
| Active operational role | 10-20% of project revenue |
| Full co-founder role | 20-30% of project revenue |

**Paid before 50/50 split:**
```
Project Revenue: €10,000
├── Stéphane (15%): €1,500
├── Remaining: €8,500
│
├── Roderic (50%): €4,250
└── Daniel (50%): €4,250
```

#### Option C: Contractor Agreement

Stéphane as independent contractor:
- Hourly rate or project fee
- Invoice → payment
- No profit sharing complexity
- Clean for taxes

**Best if:** Stéphane's involvement is limited/defined

#### Option D: "Phantom Equity" Points

- Stéphane earns points based on contribution
- Points convert to cash at exit or annually
- No actual ownership
- Common in startups

**Sources:**
- [UpCounsel: Revenue Share Agreements](https://www.upcounsel.com/revenue-share-partnership-agreement)
- [Gordon Law: Profit Sharing Agreements](https://gordonlaw.com/learn/profit-sharing-agreements/)
- [ShareWillow: Profit Sharing Template](https://www.sharewillow.com/blog/profit-sharing-agreement-template)

### My Recommendation: Option A or B

**Option A** if: Daniel and Stéphane want to figure out their own arrangement
**Option B** if: You want a clear, documented split for everyone
