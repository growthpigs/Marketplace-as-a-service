## 2. Ownership Structure

### The Situation

Roderic is **joining existing projects** that Daniel (and Stéphane) already have running:
- VIP Drivers (operational, 5,000 drivers)
- Hotel booking partnerships
- Restaurant/tourism pipeline

This means Daniel brings **existing business value**, not just future potential.

### Ownership Options

#### Option A: 50/50 (Equal Partners)

```
Rive Gosh LLC
├── Roderic Andrews ─── 50%
└── Daniel Amaury ───── 50%
```

**Rationale:**
- Equal say in all decisions
- Simple profit split
- Roderic's technical contribution = Daniel's business contribution
- Long-term alignment

**When this makes sense:**
- If Daniel values Roderic's tech skills as equal to his existing business
- If the partnership is truly a fresh start together
- If future value > current value of existing projects

#### Option B: 40/60 (Roderic/Daniel)

```
Rive Gosh LLC
├── Roderic Andrews ─── 40%
└── Daniel Amaury ───── 60%
```

**Rationale:**
- Acknowledges Daniel brings existing revenue/relationships
- Roderic builds equity through contribution over time
- Still meaningful ownership for Roderic

**When this makes sense:**
- If VIP Drivers/other projects are already generating significant revenue
- If Roderic is "joining" rather than "co-founding"

#### Option C: 50/50 with Vesting

```
Rive Gosh LLC
├── Roderic Andrews ─── 50% (vests over 24 months)
└── Daniel Amaury ───── 50% (fully vested - existing business)
```

**Rationale:**
- Roderic earns his 50% through contribution
- Protects Daniel if Roderic doesn't deliver
- Common in startups

**When this makes sense:**
- If there's uncertainty about long-term commitment
- If Daniel wants protection on existing business value

### My Recommendation: Option A (50/50)

**Why:**
1. You said "50/50" in the call - that's the intent
2. Long-term partnerships work better with equality
3. Your tech skills ARE as valuable as his business skills
4. Negotiating small percentages wastes energy and creates resentment
5. If the projects succeed, both win big; percentages matter less

**Discuss Monday:** Confirm 50/50 or if Daniel wants different split based on existing business value.

## 5. Tax Handling (French Residents)

### The Simple Version

You're all French tax residents. The US LLC is "tax transparent" (pass-through). Here's how it works:

```
US LLC (Wyoming)
│
├── No US federal tax (pass-through)
├── No US state tax (Wyoming = 0%)
│
└── Profits flow to members
    │
    ├── Roderic → French income tax
    └── Daniel → French income tax
```

### What You File

#### In the US

| Form | What | When | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Form 5472 | Foreign ownership disclosure | Apr 15 | **$25,000 penalty if missed** |
| Form 1120 | Pro-forma return | Apr 15 | No tax due, just reporting |
| Wyoming Annual Report | Renew LLC | Anniversary | $50 |

**You'll need:** A US tax preparer who handles foreign-owned LLCs. Cost: ~$300-500/year.

#### In France

| What | How |
|------|-----|
| Report LLC profits | On personal income tax (BIC or BNC category) |
| Social charges | If applicable based on activity |
| CFC rules | May apply - document that US tax = 0% |

**You've done this with Badaboost** - same process. Just report the profits on your French taxes.

### Stéphane's Tax Situation

Stéphane receives profit share = taxable income in France.
- He declares it as BNC (non-commercial profits) or miscellaneous income
- He handles his own tax obligations
- Rive Gosh provides him annual statement of payments
